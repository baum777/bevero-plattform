## Core intention

Make a Key Account Director understand the economic case before inspecting product screens: Bevero closes operational execution gaps between leading systems and daily site reality, and a small pilot can test whether that friction decreases.

## Logic followed

- Lead with the hub position and economic levers.
- State the implemented site database and adapter layer directly instead of presenting them as future-only concepts.
- Respect FoodNotify and Gastronovi as system/process solutions rather than frame them as competitors.
- Translate features into gaps, decision questions and measurable pilot indicators.
- Preserve explicit limits around project status, rollout, writeback and ROI.
- Ask for KAM assessment, not approval.

## Design assumptions

- The existing three-tab structure remains the correct information architecture.
- Six curated KAM screens remain the fastest product proof.
- ROI must stay a testable hypothesis until pilot evidence exists.
- "Integrated adapter logic" refers to repository implementation; it does not imply verified external credentials, partner endpoints, productive sync or writeback.

## Tradeoffs

- Accepted:
  - More explanatory content appears before screenshots to establish commercial relevance.
  - The ROI formula is qualitative and audit-friendly rather than numerically impressive.
- Rejected:
  - Guaranteed savings, invented benchmarks or rollout claims.
  - Competitive displacement language against FoodNotify or Gastronovi.
  - New dependencies, assets or a navigation redesign.

## Durable memory

- Bevero's landing position is: operational hub between systems and site execution.
- The dashboard is framed as database-backed and the FoodNotify/Gastronovi adapter logic as technically implemented.
- Economic value is framed through search time, shortages, rework, escalations and repeatability.
- Pilot metrics are the evidence path; they are not proof of achieved ROI.
- The CTA requests an assessment from KAM/site-quality perspective.

## Do not reuse blindly

- Do not promote the qualitative ROI formula to quantified savings without measured pilot data.
- Do not imply integrations or writebacks from the hub metaphor alone.
- Do not silently upgrade implemented Gastronovi connector code to a claim of active partner connectivity; the current contract and endpoints remain unconfirmed.

## Relation to Rauschenberger OS / Bevero

- location logic: site workflows and risks become visible and potentially comparable.
- role/approval logic: actions, approvals and audit paths remain separated.
- inventory/procurement/shift-planning logic: receipt, stock, refill and handover are presented as one operational chain.
- external-system boundary: existing systems remain leading; no automatic writeback or productive integration is claimed.
