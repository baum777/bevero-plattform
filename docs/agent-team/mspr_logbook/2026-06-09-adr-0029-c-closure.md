### 1.2 What changed in the promotion gate

The local in-memory stub tests could not catch two issues that the live-DB promotion gate surfaced. Both are fixed in the slice commit and recorded here for transparency:

1. **Check #3 regtype cast** — the verify-script initially used `'StaffRole'::regtype` (and similar for the other 2 enums) to look up the enumtypid. Postgres is **case-sensitive** in regtype casts, and the lowercased form `'staffrole'` is what Postgres stores for an unquoted enum type. The verify-script was patched to JOIN against `pg_type.typname` instead, which is case-insensitive in the catalog. 12 labels (6+3+3) confirmed.

2. **Check #11 unique composite index count** — the initial verify-script counted all 8 `UNIQUE` indexes on the 4 tables, but each table has 2: the auto-generated `_pkey` on `id` + the explicit composite business unique (e.g. `(organizationId, name)`). The check was patched to exclude `_pkey` indexes, leaving 4 business uniques as expected.

### 1.3 Documented deviations

- **Prisma-generated delegate names**: The `CUBE_EconomicDatabaseClient` interface uses Prisma-generated camelCase keys (`exclusiveRentalPolicy`, `afterMidnightStaffRate`, `nonFoodComponent`, `furniturePolicy`). This is required for the `prisma as unknown as CUBE_EconomicDatabaseClient` cast at `src/app.ts` to resolve at runtime, mirroring the 0029-B and OperationalUnit cast patterns. The deviation is naming-only; the behavior is identical.

- **FurniturePolicy conflict heuristic**: The service-layer `hasConflict` flag compares `additionalFromGuestCount` across all active policies in the org. The 00c §4 example seeds have the **same** `additionalFromGuestCount` (120) for both Website and Bankettmappe, so the heuristic returns `false` for both rows. The 00c §4 conflict is at the **interpretation** level (Website says "includiert bis 100", Bankettmappe says "Zusatzmobiliar ab 120"), not at the integer value. The authoritative conflict detection is a future ADR-0029-C.2 (synthetic `AutomationSuggestion` via the existing `/admin/automation/suggestions/:id/approve` path from ADR-0023). The service-layer heuristic is a Cockpit-Display affordance, not the security boundary.

## 5. Residual risks

1. **Partial unique on `(org, name) WHERE isActive = true`** for `ExclusiveRentalPolicy` (ADR-0029-C §Risk ID-001) — service-layer enforced; future ADR may add the partial unique index.

2. **Phantom duplicate `AfterMidnightStaffRate` rows** (ADR-0029-C §Risk ID-002) — `@@unique([organizationId, role, fromHourLocal, toHourLocal])` enforced at the DB layer.

3. **Defense-in-depth `DO $$` block fragility** (ADR-0029-C §Risk ID-004) — the block asserts trigger **presence** (count = N), not policy counts. Future slices (ADR-0029-C.2, ADR-0029-D, etc.) will widen the assertion to include their new triggers.

4. **Default Supabase privileges on `authenticated` / `anon`** (ADR-0029-C §Risk ID-006) — mitigated by the in-slice REVOKE migration. The 0029a tables (OperationalUnit/ServiceSlot/GroupRule) and the 0029b tables (CUBE_Source/CUBE_SourceField/CUBE_Conflict) have the same hygiene issue; their REVOKE migrations are commit `6017c49` (0029b) and a future follow-up (0029a).

5. **Furniture-Threshold-Konflikt** (ADR-0029-C §Risk ID-007) — the service-layer `hasConflict` heuristic is at the `additionalFromGuestCount` integer level. The 00c §4 conflict is at the **interpretation** level; the authoritative conflict detection (with a synthetic `AutomationSuggestion`) is a future ADR-0029-C.2.
